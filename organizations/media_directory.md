# Malayalam and Kerala-Focused News Media Directory

A comprehensive directory of news media outlets covering Kerala, including Malayalam
and English-language newspapers, television channels, and digital-first portals. Media
outlets are primary sources for tracking governance, policy discourse, and development
in Kerala.

**Note on verification:** Websites and social media handles were verified via web search
as of April 2026. Handles marked with "(unverified)" could not be confirmed through
official sources. Media ownership and political affiliations noted here are based on
publicly available information.

### Notes on Newspapers

- **Malayala Manorama** is the largest circulated newspaper in Kerala and 2nd largest in India (ABC 2022). Founded 1888.
- **Mathrubhumi** ("Motherland") is the 2nd most widely read daily in Kerala. Founded 1923 during the freedom struggle by K.P. Kesava Menon. Also operates Club FM radio stations and Mathrubhumi News TV.
- **Madhyamam** is India's first international newspaper (with Gulf Madhyamam editions in 7 countries). Published by Ideal Publications Trust, whose trustees have ties to Jamaat-e-Islami Hind Kerala. Also operates MediaOne TV.
- **Deshabhimani** ("Patriot") is the official organ of CPI(M) Kerala. Founded 1942. 3rd largest Malayalam daily by circulation.
- **Kerala Kaumudi** was founded in 1911. Also operates Kaumudy TV. Headquartered in Thiruvananthapuram.
- **Mangalam** was founded in 1969 in Kottayam. 6th most circulated Malayalam daily.
- **Janmabhumi** ("Motherland") was launched in 1975, affiliated with BJP/RSS. Describes itself as "Kerala's nationalistic news portal."
- **Suprabhaatham** ("Auspicious Dawn") was launched in 2014, published on behalf of Samastha Kerala Jamiyyathul Ulama (Sunni organization).
- **Chandrika** ("Crescent") started in 1932, became a daily in 1939. Mouthpiece of IUML. Has Middle East editions.
- **Thejas** was established in 2006 as a print newspaper; print editions closed in 2018, now operates as an online-only portal.

### Notes on TV Channels

- **Manorama News** launched 17 August 2006. Based at Aroor, Alappuzha. Known for programs like Counter Point (debate) and KeralaCAN (cancer support initiative).
- **Mathrubhumi News** launched 23 January 2013. 24-hour Malayalam news channel from the Mathrubhumi group.
- **Asianet News** pioneered private Malayalam news broadcasting in 1995 (first live TV news broadcast in India). Became independent 24-hour channel in 2003. Owned by Rajeev Chandrasekhar (BJP Kerala state president) through Jupiter Capital, but widely regarded for editorial independence. First Malayalam news channel to reach 10M YouTube subscribers.
- **MediaOne** launched 10 February 2013. Operated by Madhyamam Broadcasting Ltd. Based in Kozhikode. Faced a temporary government ban in 2022 over security concerns (later overturned by Supreme Court). Withdrew from BARC ratings in October 2025.
- **Reporter TV** launched 13 May 2011. Founded by M.V. Nikesh Kumar (later joined CPI(M) politics). Relaunched under new ownership (Emaraj Group) in July 2023. Based in Kochi.
- **Kairali News** (formerly People TV) launched in 2005, rebranded May 2019. Part of Malayalam Communications Ltd network along with Kairali TV, WE TV, and Kairali Arabia. Associated with CPI(M).
- **Janam TV** launched 19 April 2015. Describes itself as having a "nationalistic outlook." Politically aligned with BJP/Sangh Parivar.
- **News18 Kerala** launched 5 July 2016. Part of the Network18 franchise (owned by Reliance Industries).
- **24 News** (Twenty Four News) officially launched 8 December 2018. Promoted by TV anchor Sreekandan Nair. Second channel from Insight Media City (after Flowers TV).
- **Kaumudy TV** launched 5 May 2013. Part of the Kerala Kaumudi group.
- **Flowers News** is not a separate channel; the news arm of the Insight Media City group operates as **24 News** (listed above).

### Notes on English Media

- **The Hindu (Kerala)** has a dedicated Kerala reporting team with its own X handle (@THKerala). The most widely read English newspaper in Kerala.
- **The New Indian Express (Kerala)** maintains dedicated Kerala social media accounts using the handle "xpresskerala" across platforms.
- **Deccan Herald** does not have a dedicated Kerala edition or bureau; Kerala coverage is via tagging on their main website.
- **The News Minute** covers all five southern states from Bengaluru, with a Kerala bureau. Strong focus on investigative and explanatory journalism.
- **Onmanorama** is Malayala Manorama's English digital portal, rebranded in 2016 from English Manoramaonline (established 1998).
