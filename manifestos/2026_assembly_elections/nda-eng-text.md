1.A. FOOD SURPLUS KERALAM + FARMERS' WELFARE
The promise: Transforming Keralam into a food surplus state by reviving the agriculture
sector and improving farm incomes.
Years of negligence have compromised agriculture across the State. The Survey on
Markets in Kerala (2021-22) revealed that nearly 90% of Keralam's food grains, pulses,
vegetables, eggs and meat are sourced from other states, and so are nearly 65% of
Keralam's fruits. Whenever a neighbouring state faces a crisis, Malayalees bear the brunt
of it due to over-reliance on food imports. By empowering our farmers and building vital
storage infrastructure we will deliver high-quality, affordable food to all by transforming
Keralam into a food surplus State.
Key Initiatives
1. MSP for all crops that require support, to encourage crop diversification in the
agriculture sector, thus reducing Keralam's dependence on neighbouring states.
2. Digital Procurement and DBT for paddy farmers to replace the failed Paddy Receipt
Sheet system, guaranteeing fair, transparent and timely payments, saving them from
debt and misery.
3. State-wide Cold Storage Network Expansion to reduce post-harvest spoilage and
ensure that every Malayalee farmer can secure the best possible price for his produce.
4. Plantation Lands to have options for multicrop agriculture by amending relevant
land and forestry laws.
Initiative Impact
1. Rapid agriculture sector revival will improve farm incomes by enabling Keralam's
farmers to fairly compete for market share, while meeting local demand for quality
and affordable food.
2. Keralam will be transformed into a food surplus state with MSP and/or support price
on a wide range of crops and by freeing up plantation lands to take up multicrop
agriculture. This will incentivize local production, thus eliminating over-reliance on
neighbouring states.
3. Every Malayalee will have access to quality and affordable food, thus improving their
overall health and quality of life.

outcomes for the entire family.

2.A. DOORSTEP HEALTHCARE - MOBILE HOSPITAL
The promise: Doctors and Nurses to Your Home
The Challenge:
1. A significant share of Keralam's elderly population is bedridden or mobility-restricted.
2. Nearly one-third of OP visits are for routine follow-ups, dressing, or chronic disease
management.
3. Many elderly citizens live alone while children work abroad.
4. Routine hospital visits are physically exhausting and financially burdensome.
Healthcare must travel to the patient - not the other way around.
The Solution:
Every Block Panchayat, Municipality, and Municipal Corporation will be allocated a
dedicated Mobile Hospital Unit. Each Mobile Hospital will include:
1. A qualified Doctor
2. A trained Nurse
3. Essential medicines
4. Basic diagnostic equipment
5. Lab sample collection capability
The unit will visit residents of the respective local body and provide consultation and
essential treatment at home.
System-Wide Benefits:
1. Frees up hospital beds for critical cases
2. Reduces overcrowding in government hospitals
3. Detects complications early (diabetic ulcers, rising BP, infections)
4. Prevents costly ICU admissions
5. Reduces stress and hardship for elderly families

3. AFFORDABLE UNINTERRUPTED ENERGY FOR ALL
The promise: Providing uninterrupted and affordable energy to every household
including special subsidy for PM Surya Ghar households.
Over the last decade, Keralam's domestic electricity tariffs have steadily climbed, with
compounded increases in per-unit rates, fixed and service charges, and periodic
surcharges. These adjustments have significantly burdened the average resident,
especially middle-income families and moderate users. Repeated assurances by
Governments that there will be no power cuts in Keralam, and assertions that power cuts
are now a thing of the past have been futile, with the common man facing power outages
regularly, sometimes even unannounced.
Key Trends
1. All Round Price Hikes: Since 2015, Keralam has witnessed multiple upward rate
revisions. Slab structures have continuously shifted, as have the flat monthly fees,
regardless of consumption, coupled with unpredictable fuel cost adjustments and new
service surcharges, severely worsening the financial burden on households.
2. Barriers to Solar Adoption: While the Modi Government's PM Surya Ghar Muft Bijli
Yojana subsidizes solar panels, the potential of the scheme remains largely
underutilised due to weak implementation, and procedural hurdles orchestrated
under the state governance.
Key Initiatives
1. To ease the burden on residents, a subsidy shall be offered on all domestic electricity
charges.
2. To ensure accelerated implementation of PM Surya Ghar Muft Bijli Yojana in Keralam,
we will introduce a special state-level subsidy for households enrolling under the
scheme with the target of increasing PM Surya Ghar adoption by 5 times.
Initiative Impact
1. Immediate Financial Relief and Social Equity: An electricity subsidy translates into vital
monthly savings for middle- and lower-income families, daily wage earners, and rural
and tribal communities, ensuring that essential energy access remains a fundamental
right.
2. Every Malayalee An Energy Entrepreneur: Full and effective implementation of PM
Surya Ghar scheme will mean that every Malayale has the opportunity to not just secure
free electricity, but to sell it at a profit.

4. LPG SUBSIDY
The promise: To provide 2 free LPG cylinders per year, one each during Onam and
Christmas, to all poor households.
With about 98.1 lakh domestic LPG connections by 2025, nearly every household in
Keralam uses LPG as the primary cooking fuel. LPG cylinder expenses constitute a
significant share of overall monthly expenditure, especially for the poor and middle class
in Keralam. This makes LPG affordability a central household budget issue.
Public Health Concerns: High LPG prices may cause families to revert to firewood or
biomass, leading to increased indoor air pollution and related health risks (asthma,
respiratory illnesses) - particularly affecting women and children.
Gender Equity: An unfair strain is laid on women with the burden of cooking and fuel
collection, potentially undermining the significant progress made in women's health and
empowerment over the years.
LPG is the backbone of Keralam's cooking energy system, with its affordability central to
family well-being, public health, women's empowerment, and socioeconomic
advancement. Making LPG accessible and affordable for all households must remain a
core policy objective for Keralam's continued progress.
Understanding the criticality, every poor household shall be provided with 2 free LPG
cylinders annually, one each during Onam and Christmas.
Initiative Impact
1. Drastic Reduction in Household Expenditure: Freeing up more money in the hands of
families for essentials like food, education, and healthcare.
2. Major Public Health Improvements: Promoting LPG usage through subsidies
drastically reduces indoor air pollution, saving lives and improving maternal and child
health.
3. Women's Empowerment & Time-Saving: With affordable LPG access, they save time
and effort, freeing them to pursue income-generating work, education, or caregiving.

5. FREE WATER SUPPLY AND WATER SECURITY
The promise: Free water supply for all households and rejuvenation and conservation
of all water bodies in Keralam.
For years, Malayalees have paid the price for gross water resource mismanagement.
Rainwater harvesting systems remain underdeveloped, flood management infrastructure
is inadequate, and waterbody conservation has been neglected. This failure is now hitting
every Malayalee in daily life. In the past decade, domestic water tariffs in Keralam have
risen by over 200%, driven by policy blunders, inefficiency, and rising production and
infrastructure costs. The minimum charge for up to 10,000 litres has more than tripled
since 2011, turning basic access to water into a growing financial burden on ordinary
households.
Key Initiatives
