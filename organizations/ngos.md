# NGOs and Civil Society Organizations in Kerala

A curated directory of prominent non-governmental organizations (NGOs) and civil
society organizations actively working in Kerala, India. This list focuses on
well-established, reputable organizations with a significant presence in Kerala,
including both Kerala-origin organizations and national/international NGOs with
major Kerala operations.

Last verified: April 2026

---

## 1. Social Welfare and Poverty Alleviation

### Kudumbashree

- **Full name:** Kudumbashree (State Poverty Eradication Mission)
- **Website:** https://www.kudumbashree.org/
- **Description:** State-sponsored women-centric poverty eradication mission operating through neighbourhood groups (NHGs), area development societies, and community development societies. Runs microfinance, self-help groups, and livelihood programmes across all local bodies in Kerala.
- **Location:** Thiruvananthapuram
- **Facebook:** https://www.facebook.com/Kudumbashree/
- **X:** @Kudaborad

### Kottayam Social Service Society (KSSS)

- **Full name:** Kottayam Social Service Society
- **Website:** https://kdssonline.org/
- **Description:** Serves disadvantaged groups including widows, senior citizens, persons with disabilities, women, children, and farmers. Operates self-help groups working in community health, disaster management, rural development, and entrepreneurship development, serving approximately 60,000 families.
- **Location:** Kottayam

### Cochin Social Service Society (CSSS)

- **Full name:** Cochin Social Service Society
- **Description:** Works to advance social justice and economic growth in the Cochin region since 1969. Focuses on education, child welfare, women's empowerment, and poverty reduction to build an equitable and sustainable society.
- **Location:** Kochi (Ernakulam)

### Gandhibhavan International Trust

- **Full name:** Gandhibhavan International Trust
- **Description:** Provides charitable services including an old age home, rehabilitation centre for persons with disabilities, shelter for victims of domestic violence, mobile medical unit, and children's home.
- **Location:** Pathanapuram, Kollam

## 2. Healthcare and Public Health

### Pallium India

- **Full name:** Pallium India Trust
- **Website:** https://palliumindia.org/
- **Description:** National registered charitable trust providing free pain relief and palliative care since 2003. Runs home visit programmes, inpatient/outpatient clinics, and the Trivandrum Institute of Palliative Sciences (TIPS), designated as a WHO Collaborating Centre for Training and Policy on Access to Pain Relief. Initiated Kerala's pioneering Palliative Care Policy in 2008.
- **Location:** Thiruvananthapuram
- **Facebook:** https://www.facebook.com/palliumindia/
- **X:** @palaborumindia

### Pain and Palliative Care Society (PPCS)

- **Full name:** Pain and Palliative Care Society
- **Website:** https://www.painandpalliativecare.org/
- **Description:** A globally recognised community-based palliative care model supporting patients with chronic and terminal illnesses. Pioneered the neighbourhood network in palliative care (NNPC) model that has been replicated across Kerala and internationally.
- **Location:** Kozhikode (Calicut)

### Adarsh Foundation

- **Full name:** Adarsh Foundation
- **Description:** Founded in 1998, focuses on early identification and rehabilitation of children with disabilities. Operates 3 centres with 15 physiotherapists and 18 special educators. Approximately 55% of the children served are below 3 years and from families below the poverty line.
- **Location:** Kerala (multiple centres)

## 3. Education and Literacy

### Mitraniketan

- **Full name:** Mitraniketan Vishwavidyapeetam for Open Learning and Total Development
- **Website:** https://www.mitraniketan.org/
- **Description:** Gandhian-based organization founded in 1956 by Padmashri K. Viswanathan. Operates a nursery school, primary school, secondary school, and People's College on a 60-acre campus. Focuses on development-oriented education for disadvantaged children, youth, rural women, and marginal farmers. Also runs an ICAR Krishi Vigyan Kendra for agricultural technology transfer.
- **Location:** Vellanad, Thiruvananthapuram
- **Facebook:** https://www.facebook.com/Mitraniketan/

### Wayanad Girijana Seva Trust

- **Full name:** Wayanad Girijana Seva Trust
- **Description:** Works towards re-schooling dropouts among children from impoverished tribal (Adivasi) communities in Wayanad. Runs a residential tribal Vidyalaya at Mattilayam where children are sheltered and provided with all needs free of cost, along with vocational training.
- **Location:** Mattilayam, Wayanad

### Don Bosco Veedu Society

- **Full name:** Trivandrum Don Bosco Veedu Society
- **Description:** Part of the Salesians of Don Bosco (SDB) congregation. Since 1991, provides services to children, young people, and women at risk within Thiruvananthapuram district and across Kerala. Registered under the Travancore-Cochin Literary, Scientific and Charitable Societies Registration Act, 1955.
- **Location:** Thiruvananthapuram

### SOS Children's Villages India (Kerala)

- **Full name:** SOS Children's Villages of India
- **Website:** https://www.soschildrensvillages.in/
- **Description:** Provides family-based care, education, and support for vulnerable children. Received the KMA Award 2019 for best NGO in childcare from the Government of Kerala. First childcare NGO to receive a CRISIL rating for strong delivery capability.
- **Location:** Kochi (Cochin) and other centres in Kerala

## 4. Environment and Conservation

### Thanal

- **Full name:** Thanal Trust
- **Website:** https://thanaltrust.org/
- **Description:** Public interest research, advocacy, and education organization founded in 1986. Works on environmental health and justice, ecological agriculture, zero waste, chemical safety, biodiversity, and wildlife. Led the global campaign against endosulfan pesticide. Runs the Zero Waste Kerala initiative and strengthens tribal economies through farming and women-led livelihoods with NABARD support.
- **Location:** Kowdiar, Thiruvananthapuram
- **Facebook:** https://www.facebook.com/thanal.trust/

### ATREE (Ashoka Trust for Research in Ecology and the Environment)

- **Full name:** Ashoka Trust for Research in Ecology and the Environment
- **Website:** https://www.atree.org/
- **Description:** Globally recognised research organization focused on environmental conservation and sustainable development. Established a Community Conservation Centre in 2007 for wetland conservation at a Ramsar site lake in Alappuzha district. Works on biodiversity conservation, sustainable livelihoods, and climate action in the Western Ghats.
- **Location:** Community Conservation Centre in Alappuzha (headquarters in Bengaluru)
- **X:** @ATREEblr
- **Facebook:** https://www.facebook.com/ATREE.Bangalore/

### Nature Conservation Foundation (NCF)

- **Full name:** Nature Conservation Foundation
- **Website:** https://www.ncf-india.org/
- **Description:** Conducts research on plant and animal ecology and human impacts on wildlife across the Western Ghats including Kerala. Works to revive degraded natural ecosystems from tropical rainforests and deciduous forests to shola-grasslands. Focuses on wildlife ecology, human-wildlife interactions, and conservation policy at landscape scale.
- **Location:** Operations across Western Ghats including Kerala (headquarters in Mysuru)
- **X:** @NCFindia
- **Instagram:** @nature.conservation.foundation

### WWF-India (Kerala)

- **Full name:** World Wide Fund for Nature - India
- **Website:** https://www.wwfindia.org/
- **Description:** Active in the Western Ghats region since the early 1990s. Works to identify and map critical wildlife corridors, mitigate human-elephant conflict, strengthen protected area management, promote sustainable livelihoods, and assess status of key species like tigers and Nilgiri Tahr. Partners with CMFRI and MSC on sustainable fisheries in Kerala.
- **Location:** Operations across Kerala's Western Ghats
- **X:** @WWFIndia
- **Facebook:** https://www.facebook.com/wwfindia/

### Wildlife Conservation Society (WCS) India

- **Full name:** Wildlife Conservation Society - India
- **Website:** https://india.wcs.org/
- **Description:** Works in the Western Ghats on conservation of tigers, Asian elephants, and endemic species. Focuses on diverse ecosystems spanning wet to dry regions, addressing threats to the largest populations of key species in the region.
- **Location:** Operations in Western Ghats including Kerala (headquarters in Bengaluru)
- **X:** @WCSIndia

## 5. Women's Rights and Empowerment

### Anweshi

- **Full name:** Anweshi Women's Counselling Centre
- **Website:** https://anweshi.org/
- **Description:** Kerala's largest and oldest NGO focused on women's empowerment, active since 1993. Provides counselling, legal aid, and advocacy on issues affecting women. Works on protection, empowerment, and education of women and broader society on the benefits of gender equality.
- **Location:** Kozhikode (Calicut)

### Sakhi

- **Full name:** Sakhi Women's Resource Centre
- **Website:** https://sakhikerala.org/
- **Description:** Feminist, non-governmental, rights-based organisation. Conceived as a "space" for women, advocating for change within institutions and structures to create gender-just practices. Works towards a non-hierarchical society with gender equity, ecological perspective, and no caste or class-based exploitation.
- **Location:** Thiruvananthapuram

### RASTA

- **Full name:** Rural Agency for Social and Technological Advancement
- **Website:** https://rastaindia.org/
- **Description:** Established in 1987, committed to empowering tribes, Dalits, economically and socially disadvantaged women, and marginalized farmers. Also works on preservation of the unique natural environment of southern India.
- **Location:** Wayanad

## 7. Tribal (Adivasi) Welfare

### Nilgiris Wynaad Tribal Welfare Society (NWTWS)

- **Full name:** Nilgiris Wynaad Tribal Welfare Society
- **Website:** https://www.nwtws.org/
- **Description:** Established in 1978 to provide free medical and other care to hill tribe inhabitants in the Nilgiri and Wayanad regions. Operates a dispensary, organises health camps, runs a residential school and hostel for tribal children, and undertakes socio-economic measures for tribal upliftment.
- **Location:** Ambalamoola (between Gudalur and Sultan Bathery), Western Ghats

### HRDS India (Karshaka Project)

- **Full name:** Human Resource Development Services India
- **Website:** https://hrdsindia.org/
- **Description:** Leading NGO working for tribal welfare. Implements the Karshaka project in the Attappady region of Palakkad district to augment the forsaken land of tribal communities and retrieve traditional tribal livelihoods in Adivasi areas of Kerala.
- **Location:** Attappady, Palakkad

### Peermade Development Society (PDS)

- **Full name:** Peermade Development Society
- **Website:** https://pdspeermade.com/
- **Description:** National-level organisation established in 1980 for sustainable development of tribals, marginal and small farmers, indigenous communities, women, and children. Programmes include organic farming, watershed development, rural health, climate change, food security, and micro-enterprise development. First NGO in Kerala to receive a Tribal Development Fund from NABARD and the largest exporter of organic spices in India through its farmer consortiums of nearly 3,000 small organic spice and tea farmers.
- **Location:** Peermade, Idukki

## 8. Disability Rights and Rehabilitation

### National Institute of Speech and Hearing (NISH)

- **Full name:** National Institute of Speech and Hearing
- **Website:** https://www.nish.ac.in/
- **Description:** Society set up by the Government of Kerala in 1997 for comprehensive rehabilitation and education of persons with speech and hearing impairment. Provides RCI-approved professional courses, early intervention programmes, and audiological services. Has an integrated campus where students with hearing impairment and students with normal hearing share facilities. Accredited by NAAC with A grade.
- **Location:** Sreekariyam, Thiruvananthapuram
- **Facebook:** https://www.facebook.com/nishtvm/

### Kerala State Handicapped Persons Welfare Corporation (KSHPWC)

- **Full name:** Kerala State Handicapped Persons Welfare Corporation Limited
- **Website:** https://hpwc.kerala.gov.in/
- **Description:** Established in 1979, empowers persons with disabilities through distribution and promotion of appropriate devices and mobility aids. Follows Community Based Rehabilitation (CBR) and multisectoral approaches. Works on rehabilitation, improving living conditions, and providing support for the visually impaired, physically challenged, and persons with intellectual disabilities.
- **Location:** Thiruvananthapuram

### BUDS Institutions (Kudumbashree)

- **Full name:** BUDS Schools and Rehabilitation Centres
- **Description:** Community-based rehabilitation initiative for children and families affected by developmental disabilities, created by Kudumbashree. Originated from a 2004 pilot in Venganoor grama panchayat, Thiruvananthapuram. Now operates across multiple local bodies in Kerala, providing day-care, education, and rehabilitation for children with intellectual disabilities.
- **Location:** Multiple locations across Kerala (originated in Thiruvananthapuram)

## 9. Labour Rights and Worker Welfare

### Kerala Institute of Labour and Employment (KILE)

- **Full name:** Kerala Institute of Labour and Employment
- **Website:** https://kile.kerala.gov.in/
- **Description:** Autonomous body constituted by the Government of Kerala in 1978 for training and research in labour and allied subjects. Trains labourers, their dependents, trade union leaders, employers, and government officials. Organises training programmes, seminars, workshops, and conducts research on labour, employment, and industrial relations.
- **Location:** Thozhil Bhavan, Thiruvananthapuram
- **Facebook:** https://www.facebook.com/kile.tvm/

### South Indian Federation of Fishermen Societies (SIFFS)

- **Full name:** South Indian Federation of Fishermen Societies
- **Website:** http://www.siffs.org/
- **Description:** Apex body of organizations of small-scale artisanal fish workers. Has over 9,104 member fishermen organised through 153 primary societies in eight districts of southern peninsular India, with over 65,000 fish workers availing services. Pioneered the introduction of marine plywood boats in 1982. Provides fish marketing, technology, and welfare services.
- **Location:** Thiruvananthapuram

## 10. Human Rights and Legal Aid

### Jananeethi

- **Full name:** Jananeethi
- **Website:** http://www.jananeethi.org/
- **Description:** Provides legal aid, legal consultancy, document scrutiny, petition drafting, site visits, data collection, and monitoring of judicial proceedings. Introduced negotiated settlement through mediation and clinical legal counselling as ADR in 1992. Created India's first litigation-free village in 2000 at Thichoor village in Thrissur district.
- **Location:** Thrissur

### Human Rights Law Network (HRLN)

- **Full name:** Human Rights Law Network
- **Website:** https://hrln.org/
- **Description:** Collective of lawyers and social activists dedicated to using the legal system to advance human rights, struggle against violations, and ensure access to justice. Operates across India including Kerala.
- **Location:** Operations in Kerala (headquarters in New Delhi)

### CLAP Legal Service Institute

- **Full name:** CLAP Legal Service Institute
- **Website:** http://www.clapindia.org/
- **Description:** Non-profit NGO advancing human rights through law, legal process, and the legal system since 1982. Manages a Legal Service Institute providing legal recourse and advancing fundamental human rights with dignity.
- **Location:** India (operates in Kerala)

## 13. Science, Technology, and Research

### Kerala Sastra Sahitya Parishad (KSSP)

- **Full name:** Kerala Sasthra Sahithya Parishad (Kerala Science Literature Movement)
- **Website:** https://kssp.in/
- **Description:** Founded in 1962 as a science writers' forum, grown to approximately 60,000 members in 2,300 units across Kerala. India's largest science publisher (1,200+ books). Played a key role in the Silent Valley conservation campaign, Kerala's total literacy movement, and developing models of decentralised planning. Developed the "Kala Jatha" (Art Caravan) theatre medium for mass science communication. Won the Right Livelihood Award in 1996. Its research wing, the Integrated Rural Technology Centre (IRTC), has developed products like portable biogas plants and high-efficiency wood-burning stoves installed in over 500,000 homes.
- **Location:** Thrissur (headquarters); units across all districts
- **X:** @KSSPKerala

### Kerala State Council for Science, Technology and Environment (KSCSTE)

- **Full name:** Kerala State Council for Science, Technology and Environment
- **Website:** https://kscste.kerala.gov.in/
- **Description:** Autonomous body constituted by the Government of Kerala in 2002 (successor to STEC, established 1972). Promotes and coordinates scientific research in the state. Oversees seven research institutions including KFRI (forestry), CWRDM (water resources), NATPAC (transportation), JNTBGRI (botanical research), and ICCS (climate change studies). Advises the government on application of science and technology to development.
- **Location:** Sasthra Bhavan, Thiruvananthapuram
- **Facebook:** https://www.facebook.com/kscsteofficial/

### Centre for Development Studies (CDS)

- **Full name:** Centre for Development Studies
- **Website:** https://cds.edu/
- **Description:** Premier social science research institute established in 1971. Autonomous research institute supported by the Government of Kerala and ICSSR. Conducts research in development economics including innovation, migration, decentralisation, gender, demographics, and the Kerala development model. Offers MA and PhD programmes affiliated to Jawaharlal Nehru University. Campus designed by architect Laurie Baker.
- **Location:** Ulloor, Thiruvananthapuram
- **Facebook:** https://www.facebook.com/CdsThiruvanthapuram/

### Centre for Socio-economic and Environmental Studies (CSES)

- **Full name:** Centre for Socio-economic and Environmental Studies
- **Website:** https://csesindia.org/
- **Description:** Independent non-profit research institute founded in 1996. Conducts policy and action-oriented research, consultancy, and training. Key areas include service delivery and governance, decentralisation, labour and migration, education, marginalised communities, demographics, health, gender, and urbanisation. Serves as an Institutional National Level Monitor of the Ministry of Rural Development.
- **Location:** Padivattom, Kochi (Ernakulam)

## 14. Arts, Culture, and Heritage

### Kochi Biennale Foundation (KBF)

- **Full name:** Kochi Biennale Foundation
- **Website:** https://www.kochimuzirisbiennale.org/
- **Description:** Non-profit charitable trust established in 2010 by artists Bose Krishnamachari and Riyas Komu. Hosts the Kochi-Muziris Biennale (KMB), India's first and largest contemporary art biennale and Asia's biggest contemporary art festival. Uses an artist-curator model with exhibitions across galleries, heritage buildings, and public spaces in Fort Kochi. Also contributes to restoration of heritage properties and revival of traditional art forms. Supported by the Government of Kerala.
- **Location:** Fort Kochi, Ernakulam
- **Facebook:** https://www.facebook.com/KochiMuzirisBiennale/
- **Instagram:** @kochibiennale

### National Centre for Tangible and Intangible Cultural Heritage (NCTICH Kerala)

- **Full name:** National Centre for Tangible and Intangible Cultural Heritage
- **Website:** https://nctichkerala.org/
- **Description:** Promotes preservation, propagation, and conservation of Kerala's tangible and intangible cultural heritage. Safeguards the sanctity of Theyyam and provides financial aid to Theyyam and other ritual artists. Documents heritage arts, folklore practices, and organises fairs and festivals of craft and ritual arts. Collaborates with Union Ministry agencies, INTACH, IGNCA, and zonal cultural centres.
- **Location:** Kerala

### INTACH (Kerala Chapter)

- **Full name:** Indian National Trust for Art and Cultural Heritage - Kerala Chapter
- **Website:** https://www.intach.org/
- **Description:** Works on conserving heritage structures, documenting traditional practices, and spreading cultural awareness in Kerala. Part of the national INTACH network dedicated to preserving India's natural and cultural heritage.
- **Location:** Chapters in multiple districts across Kerala

### SPIC MACAY (Kerala Chapter)

- **Full name:** Society for the Promotion of Indian Classical Music and Culture Amongst Youth
- **Website:** https://www.spicmacay.org/
- **Description:** Promotes Indian classical music, dance, and arts among students through workshops, performances, and festivals. Active presence in Kerala schools and colleges.
- **Location:** Chapters across Kerala
