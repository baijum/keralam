# Cooperative Societies and Federations in Kerala

A comprehensive directory of major cooperative societies and federations operating in
Kerala, India. The cooperative sector is a defining feature of Kerala's economic model,
with over 1,600 primary agricultural credit societies, thousands of other cooperatives
across sectors, and membership estimated at nearly 3 crore individuals — making Kerala
one of the most cooperatively organized regions in the world.

Last verified: April 2026

---

## 1. Dairy

### Milma (Kerala Co-operative Milk Marketing Federation — KCMMF)

- **Full name:** Kerala Co-operative Milk Marketing Federation Limited (KCMMF)
- **Common abbreviation:** Milma
- **Type:** Apex cooperative federation (three-tier: primary societies > regional unions > state federation)
- **Year established:** 1980 (registered April 1980 to implement Operation Flood II)
- **Headquarters:** Thiruvananthapuram
- **Website:** https://milma.com/
- **Description:** State-level apex body for dairy cooperatives in Kerala. Oversees procurement, processing, and marketing of milk and dairy products through three regional unions and over 3,100 primary milk cooperative societies. Procures over 11 lakh litres of milk daily and sells over 16 lakh litres daily. Operates 13 milk processing plants and 8 chilling plants.
- **Key statistics:** Over 10.6 lakh farmer members across 3,102 primary milk cooperatives (as of March 2024). Kerala achieved milk self-sufficiency in 2021 under Milma's operations.
- **Facebook:** https://www.facebook.com/milmaofficial/
- **Instagram:** https://www.instagram.com/milma.kerala/
- **X:** https://x.com/MilmaOfficial

### TRCMPU (Thiruvananthapuram Regional Co-operative Milk Producers' Union)

- **Full name:** Thiruvananthapuram Regional Co-operative Milk Producers' Union Limited
- **Common abbreviation:** TRCMPU
- **Type:** Regional cooperative milk union
- **Year established:** 1985
- **Headquarters:** Ksheera Bhavan, Pattom Palace P.O., Thiruvananthapuram
- **Website:** https://milmatrcmpu.com/
- **Description:** Regional milk union serving the four southern districts of Kerala — Thiruvananthapuram, Kollam, Alappuzha, and Pathanamthitta. Handles milk procurement, processing, and distribution for the southern region under the Milma umbrella.

### ERCMPU (Ernakulam Regional Co-operative Milk Producers' Union)

- **Full name:** Ernakulam Regional Co-operative Milk Producers' Union Limited
- **Common abbreviation:** ERCMPU
- **Type:** Regional cooperative milk union
- **Year established:** 1985
- **Headquarters:** Edappally, Kochi
- **Website:** https://milmaercmpu.com/
- **Description:** Regional milk union covering the four central Kerala districts of Ernakulam, Thrissur, Kottayam, and Idukki. Manages procurement and processing of milk for the central region under the Milma federation.

### MRCMPU (Malabar Regional Co-operative Milk Producers' Union)

- **Full name:** Malabar Regional Co-operative Milk Producers' Union Limited
- **Common abbreviation:** MRCMPU
- **Type:** Regional cooperative milk union
- **Year established:** 1985
- **Headquarters:** Peringolam, Kunnamangalam P.O., Kozhikode
- **Website:** https://mrcmpu.com/
- **Description:** Regional milk union serving the six northern Kerala districts — Kasaragod, Kannur, Wayanad, Kozhikode, Malappuram, and Palakkad. Handles dairy operations for the Malabar region under the Milma network.

## 2. Banking and Finance

### Kerala Bank (Kerala State Co-operative Bank — KSCB)

- **Full name:** Kerala State Co-operative Bank Limited (branded as Kerala Bank)
- **Common abbreviation:** KSCB / Kerala Bank
- **Type:** Apex cooperative bank (scheduled bank)
- **Year established:** 1916 (as Trivandrum Central Co-operative Bank; converted to state cooperative bank in 1954; rebranded as Kerala Bank in 2019 after merger of all 13 District Co-operative Banks)
- **Headquarters:** Co-Bank Tower, Palayam, Thiruvananthapuram (corporate office in Kochi)
- **Website:** https://www.keralabank.co.in/
- **Description:** The apex cooperative bank in Kerala and the first scheduled apex cooperative bank in India. Formed through the merger of all 14 District Co-operative Banks (the final one, Malappuram DCB, merged in 2023) with the state cooperative bank, creating a two-tier cooperative banking structure. Serves as the financial backbone for approximately 1,692 Primary Agricultural Credit Societies (PACS) and 16 Urban Co-operative Banks.
- **Key statistics:** 769 branches across Kerala. Seven regional offices at Thiruvananthapuram, Alappuzha, Kottayam, Thrissur, Palakkad, Kozhikode, and Kannur.
- **Instagram:** https://www.instagram.com/keralabankonline/

### KSCARDB (Kerala State Co-operative Agricultural and Rural Development Bank)

- **Full name:** Kerala State Co-operative Agricultural and Rural Development Bank Limited
- **Common abbreviation:** KSCARDB / KSCARD Bank
- **Type:** Apex cooperative bank (long-term agricultural credit)
- **Year established:** 1956 (origins trace to 1931 State Land Mortgage Bank in Travancore)
- **Headquarters:** Thiruvananthapuram
- **Website:** https://keralalandbank.in/
- **Description:** Apex body of primary cooperative agricultural and rural development banks in Kerala, catering to long-term agricultural credit needs. Has 63 primary cooperative agricultural and rural development banks as members functioning at the taluk level. Offers 23 loan product lines across farm, non-farm, housing, and short-term categories. Recognized as the best long-term cooperative bank in India.

### Primary Agricultural Credit Societies (PACS)

- **Type:** Primary cooperative societies (grassroots level)
- **Description:** The base-level institutions of Kerala's short-term cooperative credit structure. Unlike other Indian states, Kerala has exceptionally high dependency on cooperative credit institutions, with about 60% of the population holding PACS membership.
- **Key statistics:** Approximately 1,606 to 1,692 PACS functioning across Kerala, with a membership of about 2.29 crore individuals. These societies form the foundation for Kerala Bank's deposit and lending operations.

### KSFE (Kerala State Financial Enterprises)

- **Full name:** Kerala State Financial Enterprises Limited
- **Common abbreviation:** KSFE
- **Type:** Government-owned company (not a cooperative; included here due to its cooperative-adjacent role in financial inclusion)
- **Year established:** 1969 (commenced operations 6 November 1969)
- **Headquarters:** Thrissur
- **Website:** https://ksfe.com/
- **Description:** Fully state government-owned miscellaneous non-banking company, primarily operating chitty (chit fund) schemes. Established to provide an alternative to private-sector chit fund organizers. One of only two government-owned chit fund companies in India (the other being MSIL in Karnataka). Holds approximately 77% of the capital volume of chit fund business in Kerala.
- **Key statistics:** Over 680 branches, 1 digital business centre, and 16 regional offices. Started with a paid-up capital of Rs 2 lakhs and 45 employees in 1969.
- **Facebook:** https://www.facebook.com/KSFE.Ltd.Official/
- **Instagram:** https://www.instagram.com/ksfeltdofficial/
- **X:** https://x.com/ksfe_ltd
- **Note:** KSFE is a Government of Kerala undertaking (PSU), not a cooperative society. It is included in this directory due to its significant role in Kerala's financial ecosystem alongside the cooperative sector.

## 3. Agricultural Marketing

### Marketfed (Kerala State Co-operative Marketing Federation)

- **Full name:** Kerala State Co-operative Marketing Federation Limited
- **Common abbreviation:** Marketfed
- **Type:** Apex cooperative marketing federation
- **Year established:** 1942 (as a Malabar-based district wholesale cooperative store; became apex state-level federation on 10 August 1960)
- **Headquarters:** Maveli Road, Gandhi Nagar, Kadavanthra, Kochi
- **Website:** https://marketfed.com/
- **Description:** The apex marketing organization in Kerala's cooperative sector, playing a pivotal role in agricultural marketing, procurement, and distribution. Premier wholesale distributor of fertilizers, partnering with national entities like IFFCO and FACT. Serves as a Cluster-Based Business Organization (CBBO) for Farmer Producer Organizations (FPOs) under the Ministry of Agriculture.
- **Key statistics:** 93 members comprising the Kerala Government and primary agricultural marketing societies. Over 80-year legacy in agricultural cooperative marketing.
- **Facebook:** https://www.facebook.com/keralamarketfed/

## 4. Consumer and Retail

### Consumerfed (Kerala State Co-operative Consumers' Federation)

- **Full name:** The Kerala State Co-operatives Consumers' Federation Limited
- **Common abbreviation:** Consumerfed
- **Type:** Apex cooperative federation (consumer cooperatives)
- **Year established:** 1965 (registered 4 September 1965; commenced operations 7 October 1965)
- **Headquarters:** Gandhi Nagar, Ernakulam, Kochi
- **Website:** https://consumerfed.net/
- **Description:** Apex body of consumer cooperatives in Kerala, established to protect consumers from retail middleman exploitation. Operates the Triveni Supermarket chain, Neethi Medical Stores (medicines at 18–70% below MRP), LPG distribution under Neethi Gas, Sushrusha clinical laboratories, and beverages retail. The first cooperative federation in Kerala to receive e-governance status.
- **Key statistics:** 357 retail outlets, over 2,284 staff, annual turnover above Rs 2,887 crore (2023–24). Board of 18 directors representing 14 district wholesale stores, 3 government nominees, and the Managing Director.
- **Facebook:** https://www.facebook.com/people/Team-Consumerfed/100054491048953/

### Triveni Supermarkets

- **Full name:** Triveni Supermarkets (operated by Consumerfed)
- **Type:** Cooperative retail chain
- **Year established:** Part of Consumerfed operations
- **Headquarters:** Thiruvananthapuram (operated under Consumerfed, headquartered in Kochi)
- **Website:** https://consumerfed.net/triveni/
- **Description:** Chain of cooperative retail supermarkets operated by Consumerfed across Kerala. The name "Triveni" represents the confluence of three values — Quality, Low Cost, and Reliability. Offers affordable grocery products, household essentials, and consumer goods through urban and semi-urban locations across the state.
- **Key statistics:** 165 Triveni Super Markets and 33 Triveni Godowns, plus Little Triveni Super Stores and Mega Marts — totalling approximately 183 outlets statewide.

### Supplyco (Kerala State Civil Supplies Corporation)

- **Full name:** Kerala State Civil Supplies Corporation Limited
- **Common abbreviation:** Supplyco
- **Type:** Public Sector Undertaking (fully government-owned company — NOT a cooperative)
- **Year established:** 1974
- **Headquarters:** Gandhi Nagar, Kochi
- **Website:** https://supplycokerala.com/
- **Description:** Government-owned corporation that regulates market prices of essential commodities. Operates the Maveli Stores chain and Supplyco supermarkets. Distributes pulses, spices, and other essentials at government-subsidised prices through market intervention programmes.
- **Key statistics:** Over 1,600 outlets including Maveli Stores, supermarkets, and depots across 56 taluks. Over 4,500 staff.
- **Note:** Supplyco is a PSU (Public Sector Undertaking), not a cooperative society. It is included here for reference as it is sometimes confused with the cooperative consumer retail sector.

## 5. Manufacturing and Labour

### Kerala Dinesh Beedi (Kerala Dinesh Beedi Workers' Central Co-operative Society)

- **Full name:** Kerala Dinesh Beedi Workers' Central Co-operative Society Limited
- **Common abbreviation:** Kerala Dinesh / Dinesh Beedi / KDB
- **Type:** Workers' production cooperative (central cooperative society)
- **Year established:** 1969 (registered 15 February 1969)
- **Headquarters:** Payyambalam, Kannur
- **Website:** https://www.keraladinesh.com/
- **Description:** One of the largest industrial cooperatives in India, formed when over 12,000 beedi workers lost their jobs after Mangalore Ganesh Beedi closed operations. Workers mobilised Re 1 each to form the cooperative, which grew to employ nearly 42,000 workers at its peak (1969–1990). Has since diversified from beedi manufacturing into food products (Dinesh Foods — coconut milk, curry powder, virgin coconut oil, pickles), garments (Dinesh Apparel), and IT services (Dinesh Information Technology Systems / DITS, which established Dinesh Software Park in Kannur in 2001). All food products are HACCP, FSSAI, and ISO 9001 certified.
- **Key statistics:** Currently provides direct employment to approximately 6,000 people across Kannur, Kasargod, and Kozhikode districts. Won the ASSOCHAM Fair Business Practice Award from 2014 to 2019.
- **Facebook:** https://www.facebook.com/Keraladineshbeedi/
- **Instagram:** https://www.instagram.com/kerala_dinesh/
- **Online store:** https://shoppydin.com/

### Dinesh Foods

- **Full name:** Dinesh Foods (division of Kerala Dinesh Beedi Workers' Central Co-operative Society)
- **Type:** Food processing division of a workers' cooperative
- **Headquarters:** Kannur
- **Website:** https://www.keraladinesh.com/Foods/
- **Description:** Food processing division of Kerala Dinesh, launched in the late 1990s as part of the cooperative's diversification away from tobacco. Manufactures spice powders, coconut milk, virgin coconut oil, coconut milk ice cream, fruit squash, fruit jam, syrup, and pickles. Employs former beedi workers. HACCP, FSSAI, AGMARK, ISO 9001, and SA 8000 certified.

### ULCCS (Uralungal Labour Contract Co-operative Society)

- **Full name:** Uralungal Labour Contract Co-operative Society Limited
- **Common abbreviation:** ULCCS
- **Type:** Workers' cooperative (labour contract cooperative — primary society)
- **Year established:** 1925 (founded 13 February 1925)
- **Headquarters:** Madappally College P.O., Vatakara, Kozhikode, Kerala
- **Website:** https://ulccsltd.com/
- **Description:** Asia's largest and the world's second-largest workers' cooperative (ranked 2nd in 2021 World Cooperative Monitor for industry and utilities). Founded by 14 workers with a capital of 37 paise under the guidance of social reformer Vagbhatananda Guru. Specializes in construction for government departments (PWD, National Highway, Irrigation, Tourism). Has diversified through subsidiaries including UL Cyberpark (the first Green LEED gold-rated building by a cooperative), UL Technology Solutions, Sargaalaya Arts and Crafts Village, UL Education, UL Agriculture, and UL Housing.
- **Key statistics:** Annual turnover over Rs 1,400 crore. Assets exceeding Rs 6,600 crore. Direct workforce of over 18,000 workers plus 1,500 engineers. Over 7,500 projects completed. Celebrated its centenary in 2025.
- **Facebook:** https://www.facebook.com/ulccsltd/
- **Instagram:** https://www.instagram.com/ulccs_ltd/
- **X:** https://x.com/ULCCSLtd

## 6. Coir

### Coirfed (Kerala State Co-operative Coir Marketing Federation)

- **Full name:** The Kerala State Co-operative Coir Marketing Federation Limited
- **Common abbreviation:** Coirfed
- **Type:** Apex cooperative federation (coir sector)
- **Year established:** 1979
- **Headquarters:** Ravi Karanakaran Road, Alappuzha
- **Website:** http://www.coirfedindia.in/
- **Description:** Apex federation of primary coir cooperatives in Kerala, based in Alappuzha — the coir capital of India. Procures and markets products from its affiliated coir cooperatives. Manufactures rubberised coir products (mattresses, PVC tufted mats, coir mattings, coir tiles, rubber-backed mats) as well as coir fibre, coir pith, pith briquettes, coir pith compost (organic manure), coir geo-textiles, grow bags, and coir fibre garden pots.
- **Key statistics:** 734 affiliated primary coir cooperatives. Four regional offices (Kollam, Kochi, Kozhikode, Alappuzha) for coir yarn procurement and distribution across 80+ varieties. 47 showrooms and over 100 retail outlets. Board of 23 directors.
- **Facebook:** https://www.facebook.com/CoirfedKerala/

## 7. Handloom and Textiles

### Hantex (Kerala State Handloom Weavers' Co-operative Society)

- **Full name:** Kerala State Handloom Weavers' Co-operative Society Limited (No. H.232)
- **Common abbreviation:** Hantex
- **Type:** Apex cooperative society (handloom sector)
- **Year established:** 1961 (formed by amalgamating three central cooperative societies from Malabar, Cochin, and Travancore regions)
- **Headquarters:** Kaithary Bhavan Building, Oottukuzhy, Thiruvananthapuram
- **Website:** https://www.hantex.kerala.gov.in/
- **Description:** Apex body of primary handloom weavers' cooperative societies in Kerala. Markets products of member societies and provides institutional support to handloom weavers. Operates two weaving factories and a garments unit with modern machinery. Products include handcrafted fabrics, garments, furnishings, sarees, and traditional wears adhering to eco-friendly norms.
- **Key statistics:** 523 registered handloom societies with over 20,000 handloom weavers. 76 showrooms across Kerala. Average annual turnover of approximately Rs 35 crore. Four regional offices and six production centres.
- **Facebook:** https://www.facebook.com/p/Hantex-100086645221814/
- **Instagram:** https://www.instagram.com/hantex_kerala/

### Hanveev (Kerala State Handloom Development Corporation)

- **Full name:** Kerala State Handloom Development Corporation Limited
- **Common abbreviation:** Hanveev
- **Type:** State government company (NOT a cooperative)
- **Year established:** 1968 (incorporated 24 June 1968)
- **Headquarters:** Kannur (corporate office); regional offices at Ernakulam and Thiruvananthapuram
- **Website:** http://hanveevs.com/
- **Description:** State government-owned corporation focused on preserving traditional handloom weaving and protecting handloom artisans from competition by power looms. Distinct from Hantex (which is the cooperative entity). Both Hanveev and Hantex operate under the Directorate of Handlooms and Textiles.
- **Key statistics:** 32 production centres engaging 1,950 skilled handloom artisans. 36 exclusive showrooms and 2 agency showrooms.
- **Note:** Hanveev is a state government company, not a cooperative society. It is included here for reference as it works alongside the cooperative handloom sector.

## 8. Rubber and Plantation

### RubberMark (Kerala State Co-operative Rubber Marketing Federation)

- **Full name:** Kerala State Co-operative Rubber Marketing Federation Limited
- **Common abbreviation:** RubberMark
- **Type:** Apex cooperative federation (rubber marketing)
- **Year established:** 1971
- **Headquarters:** P.B. No. 15, Gandhi Nagar, Kadavanthra, Kochi
- **Website:** https://www.rubbermark.com/
- **Description:** Apex federation of primary rubber marketing and processing cooperative societies in Kerala. The only government agency in India that procures natural rubber directly from farmers. Provides fertilizers, planting materials, pesticides, chemicals, manures, and agricultural implements to rubber farmers. Also manufactures and exports rubber products through units including Rubek Balloons, Crumb Rubber Factory (Chenappady), Intermix Factory (Kaduthuruthy), and RubberMark Rubber Industries.
- **Key statistics:** 38 member primary rubber marketing and processing cooperative societies across Kerala. Largest supplier of natural rubber in India. Active participation of Rubber Board and Government of Kerala in governance.

### Rubco (Kerala State Rubber Co-operative Limited)

- **Full name:** Kerala State Rubber Co-operative Limited
- **Common abbreviation:** Rubco
- **Type:** State cooperative (rubber manufacturing and marketing)
- **Year established:** 1997
- **Headquarters:** Valiyavelicham KSIDC Industrial Estate, Kuthuparambu, Kannur
- **Website:** (no dedicated website identified)
- **Description:** Established by the Kerala government to stabilise rubber prices and directly procure natural rubber from local farmers. Has evolved into a diversified conglomerate manufacturing and marketing rubber-based products (footwear, mats, mattresses), rubber compounds for the tyre industry, furniture, virgin coconut oil, and other products. Started with five initial units and ventured into Hawaii chappal production (3 lakh pairs annually) in 1999.
- **Key statistics:** Rubber compound mixing plant with 2,200 MT monthly capacity, supplying to the Indian tyre industry.
- **Facebook:** https://www.facebook.com/rubcogroup/

## 9. Fisheries

### Matsyafed (Kerala State Co-operative Federation for Fisheries Development)

- **Full name:** Kerala State Co-operative Federation for Fisheries Development Limited
- **Common abbreviation:** Matsyafed
- **Type:** Apex cooperative federation (fisheries sector)
- **Year established:** 1984 (registered 19 March 1984)
- **Headquarters:** Kamaleswaram, Manacaud P.O., Thiruvananthapuram
- **Website:** https://matsyafed.in/
- **Description:** Apex federation of primary-level fishermen development welfare cooperative societies. Promotes economic and social development of the fishermen community through production, procurement, processing, and marketing of fish and fish products. Supplies quality fishing inputs at subsidised rates. Sole importer and distributor of Suzuki outboard motors in South India. Operates fish farms with eco-friendly aquatourism facilities at Njarakkal, Malippuram, and Palaikkari. Has constructed over 33,400 houses for fishing community members.
- **Key statistics:** 651 affiliated primary fishermen development welfare cooperative societies (335 marine, 198 inland, 118 women cooperatives). Total membership over 3 lakh. Paid-up share capital Rs 150 crore. Board of 22 directors. Operates net factory at Ernakulam (established 1966) using Japanese technology.
- **Facebook:** https://www.facebook.com/matsyafed/

- **Common abbreviation:** Housefed

- **Year established:** 1970

## Clarifications on Non-Cooperative Entities

The following entities are sometimes associated with Kerala's cooperative sector but are
actually government-owned companies (PSUs) or corporations, not cooperatives:

| Entity | Actual Type | Notes |
|--------|-------------|-------|
| **Supplyco** (Kerala State Civil Supplies Corporation) | PSU (fully government-owned company, incorporated 1974) | Consumer retail; operates Maveli Stores chain |
| **KSFE** (Kerala State Financial Enterprises) | PSU (government-owned MNBC, established 1969) | Chit fund and financial services |
| **Hanveev** (Kerala State Handloom Development Corporation) | State government company (incorporated 1968) | Handloom weaving; distinct from Hantex (cooperative) |
