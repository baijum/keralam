# 2016 Kerala Legislative Assembly Election Results

**Election Date:** 16 May 2016
**Results Declared:** 19 May 2016
**Total Constituencies:** 140 (14 SC-reserved, 2 ST-reserved, 124 General)
**Voter Turnout:** 77.53%

The 2016 election saw the Left Democratic Front (LDF) sweep back to power,
defeating the incumbent United Democratic Front (UDF) government. The BJP-led
NDA opened its account in the Kerala Assembly for the first time, with O.
Rajagopal winning the Nemom seat. Pinarayi Vijayan was sworn in as Chief
Minister on 25 May 2016.

## Notable Results

- **Highest victory margin:** P. J. Joseph (KC(M)), Thodupuzha -- 45,587 votes
- **Lowest victory margin:** Anil Akkara (INC), Wadakkanchery -- 43 votes
- **Chief Minister:** Pinarayi Vijayan (CPI(M)), won from Dharmadom with a margin of 36,905
- **Leader of Opposition:** Ramesh Chennithala (INC), won from Haripad with a margin of 18,621
- **Nemom:** O. Rajagopal (BJP) became the first BJP MLA ever in the Kerala Assembly, winning by 8,671 votes
- **Narrowest margins:** Wadakkanchery (43), Manjeshwaram (89), Peerumade (314)
- **Veteran wins:** V. S. Achuthanandan (CPI(M)), the 93-year-old former Chief Minister, won from Malampuzha by 27,142 votes; Oommen Chandy (INC) won from Puthuppally by 27,092 votes; K. M. Mani (KC(M)) won from Pala by 4,703 votes
