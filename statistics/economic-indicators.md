# Kerala Economic and Development Indicators

A compilation of verified economic, employment, infrastructure, fiscal, and
sectoral data for the state of Kerala, sourced from the Kerala Economic Review,
RBI, MOSPI, PLFS, NITI Aayog, TRAI, and other authoritative publications.

Demographics (population, literacy, health, HDI) are covered separately in
`demographics.md`.

**Key observations:**
- Kerala's youth unemployment rate (29.9%) is nearly three times the national
  average (10.2%).
- Female youth unemployment at 47.1% is especially alarming.
- The education-unemployment paradox: Despite near-100% youth literacy and
  41.3% GER in higher education (vs 28.4% national average), Kerala has
  among the highest youth NEET (Not in Education, Employment, or Training)
  rates at 26%.
- Kerala had the highest migration rate among all Indian states in 2021
  (ILO, 2024). About 2.2 million people have emigrated abroad and 0.5
  million to other Indian states.
- More than half of the workforce is engaged in the informal sector.

### Extreme Poverty Eradication

On 1 November 2025, Kerala became the first Indian state officially declared
free from extreme poverty. The Extreme Poverty Eradication Programme, launched
in 2021, identified 64,006 extremely poor families through detailed surveys.
Micro-plans were prepared for each family, ensuring access to housing, land,
and documents. As of 2025, 59,277 families have been successfully uplifted.

### MPI Context

| Indicator | Value | Source |
|---|---|---|
| India MPI value (Global MPI 2025) | 0.069 | Global MPI Report 2025 |
| Persons exiting MPI poverty (2013-14 to 2022-23) | 24.82 crore | NITI Aayog 2024 |
| India MPI decline | 29.17% to 11.28% | NITI Aayog 2024 |

Kerala, along with Tamil Nadu and Himachal Pradesh, has the lowest
multidimensional poverty levels in India, reflecting better education, health,
and infrastructure outcomes.

### Kudumbashree Impact on Poverty

According to a study by the Centre for Development Studies (CDS),
Thiruvananthapuram, Kudumbashree reduced the incidence of poverty in Kerala
by 26 percentage points between 1998 and 2018.

  in Central government transfers.

## Data Notes

- **(P)** = Provisional estimate; **(Q)** = Quick estimate; **(RE)** = Revised
  estimate; **(BE)** = Budget estimate.
- GSVA (Gross State Value Added) differs from GSDP by excluding net product
  taxes. Sectoral shares are typically reported on GSVA basis.
- PLFS state-level data for LFPR and WPR specific to Kerala for 2023-24
  require the detailed annexure tables of the PLFS Annual Report.
- Banking data (total deposits, credit, CD ratio) for 2023-24 is published
  in RBI Quarterly Statistics and Kerala Economic Review 2024 but specific
  figures were not freely available during compilation.
