# Kerala Demographics Reference

A compilation of verified demographic data for the state of Kerala, sourced from
the Census of India 2011, Kerala Economic Review, NFHS-5, Sample Registration
System (SRS), and NITI Aayog publications.

## 3. Historical Population (Census 1951-2011)

| Census Year | Population | Decadal Growth Rate (%) |
|---|---|---|
| 1951 | 1,35,49,154 | — |
| 1961 | 1,69,03,715 | 24.76 |
| 1971 | 2,13,47,375 | 26.29 |
| 1981 | 2,54,53,680 | 19.24 |
| 1991 | 2,90,98,518 | 14.32 |
| 2001 | 3,18,41,374 | 9.42 |
| 2011 | 3,34,06,061 | 4.86 |

Source: Census of India; India Budget Statistical Appendix (indiabudget.gov.in);
Kerala State Planning Board Economic Review 2016-2017

Kerala's decadal growth rate has declined consistently since 1971. The 2001-2011
growth rate of 4.86% is the lowest recorded since independence and is far below
the national average of 17.64%.

## 4. Literacy (Census 2011)

| Indicator | Kerala | India (National Average) |
|---|---|---|
| Overall literacy rate | 93.91% | 74.04% |
| Male literacy rate | 96.11% | 82.14% |
| Female literacy rate | 92.07% | 65.46% |
| Rank among states | 1st | — |

Source: Census of India 2011

Kerala has been the most literate state in India since the 1991 Census. The
female literacy rate in Kerala exceeds the national male literacy rate. Even
the lowest district literacy rate in Kerala (Palakkad, 88.49%) is higher than
the national average (74.04%).

## 5. Sex Ratio (Census 2011)

| Indicator | Kerala | India (National Average) |
|---|---|---|
| Overall sex ratio (females per 1,000 males) | 1,084 | 940 |
| Child sex ratio (0-6 years) | 959 | 914 |
| Urban sex ratio | 1,091 | — |
| Rural sex ratio | 1,078 | — |

Source: Census of India 2011

Kerala is the only state in India where females outnumber males. The overall
sex ratio improved from 1,058 in 2001 to 1,084 in 2011. The child sex ratio,
however, declined from 965 in 2001 to 959 in 2011.

The child population (0-6 years) in 2011 was 34,72,955, constituting 10.4%
of the total population.

Hindus form the majority in all districts except Malappuram, where Muslims

Christians outside the northeastern states.

## 8. Key Development Indicators: Kerala vs. India

| Indicator | Kerala | India | Source |
|---|---|---|---|
| HDI (Human Development Index) | 0.758 | 0.644 | Global Data Lab 2022; UNDP HDR 2023-24 |
| Life expectancy at birth (male) | ~72 years | ~68 years | SRS Abridged Life Tables 2014-18 |
| Life expectancy at birth (female) | ~78 years | ~71 years | SRS Abridged Life Tables 2014-18 |
| Infant Mortality Rate (IMR) | 4.4 per 1,000 live births | 35 per 1,000 live births | NFHS-5 (2019-21) |
| IMR (SRS latest) | 5 per 1,000 live births | 25 per 1,000 live births | SRS 2021 |
| Maternal Mortality Ratio (MMR) | 30 per 1,00,000 live births | 88 per 1,00,000 live births | SRS Special Bulletin 2021-23 |
| Total Fertility Rate (TFR) | 1.5 | 2.0 | SRS 2021 |
| Under-5 Mortality Rate (U5MR) | 5.2 per 1,000 live births | 42 per 1,000 live births | NFHS-5 (2019-21) |
| U5MR (SRS latest) | 8 per 1,000 live births | 31 per 1,000 live births | SRS 2021 |
| Neonatal Mortality Rate (NMR) | 4 per 1,000 live births | 19 per 1,000 live births | SRS 2021 |
| NITI Aayog SDG Index Score | 79/100 (Rank 1) | — | NITI Aayog SDG India Index 2023-24 |
| NITI Aayog Health Index Score | 82.20 (Rank 1) | — | NITI Aayog Health Index 2019 |

**Notes:**
- Kerala's IMR of 5 per 1,000 live births is five times lower than the national
  average and comparable to developed countries (US IMR: 5.6).
- Kerala's MMR increased from 20 to 30 per 1,00,000 live births (SRS 2021-23),
  partly due to declining live births raising the per-100,000 ratio. Despite
  this rise, Kerala and Andhra Pradesh share the lowest MMR among Indian states.
- Kerala's TFR of 1.5 has been below the replacement level (2.1) since 1991.
- Nearly 100% of births in Kerala are institutional (hospital) deliveries.

## 9. Age Structure

| Age Group | Kerala (%) | India (%) | Source |
|---|---|---|---|
| 0-14 years | ~23% | ~29% | Census of India 2011; SRS 2021 |
| 15-59 years | ~64% | ~62% | Census of India 2011; SRS 2021 |
| 60+ years | 12.6% | 8.0% | Census of India 2011 |

Source: Census of India 2011; Kerala State Planning Board Economic Review 2017;
SRS Statistical Report 2021

| Dependency Indicator | Kerala | India | Source |
|---|---|---|---|
| Old-age dependency ratio | 19.6% | — | Census of India 2011 |
| Elderly population (60+) share | 12.6% | 8.0% | Census of India 2011 |
| Elderly population share (SRS 2021) | 14.4% | 9.0% | SRS 2021 |

**Key observations:**
- Kerala has the highest proportion of elderly (60+) population among all
  Indian states.
- The proportion of children (0-14) declined from 43% in 1961 to 23% in 2011.
- Child population (0-6 years) showed negative growth (-8.44%) between
  2001 and 2011.
- Kerala's old-age dependency ratio of 19.6% is projected to rise to 34.3%
  by 2031 (Kerala State Planning Board).
- Kerala is characterized as "India's oldest state" demographically, a
  consequence of its early achievement of low fertility and high life expectancy.
